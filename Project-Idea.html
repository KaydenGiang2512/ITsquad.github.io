OVERVIEW

  As we are approaching the concluding weeks of our first semester here at RMIT, it can be assumed that our team has a relatively clear understanding of the ever-advancing IT field and what is expected of us, such as the skills we need to acquire and the methods to effectively succeed in this industry. In addition, the feedback from our previous assignments also served as an invaluable guide that helped us define our strengths and weaknesses during the whole process of choosing a project topic suitable to our abilities. As a result, after much deliberation and discussion between potential options, our group having collectively decided on programming a simple trivia game for entertainment purposes – Ultimate Trivia game.

  The general idea that our team is going for is to construct a multiple-choice game that contains questions about various topics and displays four options from which the user can choose. By selecting the correct answer, the game will award users with a specific number of points per question and vice versa, deduct the same amount should they choose the incorrect answers. Along the way, there will be lifelines to assist the user when they encounter a difficult question and each lifeline can only be used once. At the end of a 10-question round, the window will return the total number of points the user managed to get along with some encouraging text. We also have intentions of adding an all-time high scores board so that players can compare their scores and compete against their friends, thereby increasing the level of excitement this game brings to the users.

  The outcomes our team are hoping to achieve is a captivating game with an aesthetically-pleasing interface that not only serves as a form of leisure but also as an educational platform where people can learn interesting facts and useful knowledge that they wouldn’t have discovered otherwise. Moreover, we want to bring a fun, repetitive game with no consequences or fierce competition into the gaming scene for all ages to benefit from and enjoy. The questions in our game will be creative, fascinating, factual yet some of them is surprisingly distant from the general public, in order to bring new knowledge into our users’ perception. The will be no limits to the variety of the questions therefore people need to be knowledgeable about many different topics to get all of them right.

  The biggest factor that influenced our choice for this capstone project is derived from our mutual passion and great interest in creating a functional game. Even though we took the time to carefully consider several of the possible options for this project, such as a healthcare app, a web app, AI/VR applications or a self-study app, our team still came around to the initial suggestion of game development and ultimately went forward with this idea. Aside from the mentioned statement, we figured that this particular topic was quite suitable to the current skillset of each individual and also fits the allotted timeframe of this assignment, so we should be able to complete the main functionalities of this game by the deadline due to the fact that this project is reasonably difficult             but is not entirely impossible. 

  This trivia game that we are planning to create is intriguing to us for several reasons, one of which being the practicality that this game brings. Since its main purpose is to ask the player trivial questions about diverse topics, the game provides users with an opportunity to receive new knowledge in a cheerful, interactive and voluntary manner, as opposed to reading these facts online. Additionally, this project idea is an interesting challenge that our team wants to tackle, simply because we are curious to see our favorite childhood game recreated in a unique way that we desire it to be. With this idea in mind, we are attempting to re-popularize questionnaire games, a genre that is gradually fading compared to strategic/combat games with 3D graphics such as League of Legends, PUBG, Clash of Clans or Call of Duty. In essence, we want to show people that trivia games are not obsolete or out of style but rather a fun way to relieve stress and gain new understanding about things previously unknown to them.

    If a future employer were to scrutinize our project, that person will see the amount of dedication we had for programming this game, the good organization/structure of the final product and most importantly the adequate collaboration/communication between group members to ensure team success  

  Prior to taking up this assignment, we realized that the idea itself is not exactly innovative or game-changing by any means, since a great number of games out there have already implemented this concept to some extent. A few notable mentions include “Who wants to be a millionaire?”, “Jeopardy”, “Trivia Crack”, “Trivial Pursuit”, “Quiz Up” and “Trivia 360”. These are examples of apps that uses the trivial questions module to trigger curiosity, consequently inviting more users to try the game for themselves and test their field of knowledge. Our “Ultimate Trivia game” will be implementing these particular strategies to attract our users and therefore will be more or less similar to its predecessors, but the aspect that sets us apart from the rest of the trivia landscape mainly relies on the format of this game, where you will not have to worry about lives, but rather focus on getting as many precise answers as you can. Detailed specifications of the mechanism of our game as well as characteristics will be explained in the following parts.


Detailed description

  Essentially, our game is a platform freely accessible for all demographics regardless of age, gender, abilities and most of all, intelligence. This will be like a fun test of how much you know about general knowledge, academic knowledge, cultural knowledge or even obscure facts/statistics that are virtually hidden to the vast majority. To further elaborate, the primary objective of our “Ultimate Trivia game” is to provide users with a captivating yet useful method to pass the time where we as the developers facilitate accurate information in the form of a questionnaire game, and our users are the players who will attempt to precisely answer as many questions as they possibly can and eventually receive their total points at the end of a round. Throughout the entire experience with this game, players will encounter different questions of varying difficulties, and they will only have approximately 30 seconds to choose the right answer from four possible options. If they select the correct choice, a congratulations message will appear on the window along with a certain amount of rewarded points before they move on to the following question, and if the second scenario occurs in which the user chooses one of the three incorrect options, another message will be displayed on the screen saying what the right answer was and wishing them good luck on the next question. This process will be repeating itself until the round is finished, where the program will then calculate the accumulated points and return the results for the users to view how well they did. That is the general aim that any typical trivia game would have, and our version will also try to carry this ethic as well as adding distinguishable features that separates us from previous games of the same genre.
The overall goal for our team in this project is clear, so now we need to divide this gigantic project into smaller objectives and then we can go through each milestone one by one until the checklist is complete. The first of which is to build a proper user interface for our users to interact with including a vibrant background, a question box containing the prompt and 4 answer boxes each showing one of the potential choices. Other elements might be a 30-second countdown located in the top of the screen and the lifelines on the top left corner displaying the methods to guide you through a tough question. Overall, our first agenda during the process of conducting this project is to create an eye-catching interface with a simple design suitable to a wide demographic of all age groups.

   Our second objective is to fill up this game with the most interesting questions from different fields to make our game as diverse as we possibly can, exponentially increasing the level of excitement for users. When it comes to selecting the appropriate questions, our team will be evaluating them based on a series of criteria, ranging from complexity/difficulty, topic, obscurity, humor, popularity, legitimacy and so much more. Our main sources of information would most likely be Google, Wikipedia, YouTube due to the fact that these are some of the world’s largest collection of knowledge and we are all quite familiar with these search engines. However, finding good information is not easy at all since the Internet is incredibly vast, which is why we will narrow those questions down by topic/category to make the process less time-consuming and much easier to keep track. For instance, we would look for the best questions in each field respectively, such as healthcare, culture/food, schools/universities, science, technology, riddles, math, literature, geography, sports, celebrities, music, etc. Once we determine that our team has collected enough questions in a specific field, we will continue on to the next area. As a result, users can expect a challenging game of knowledge with lots of facts they might never have heard of before.
  Finally, our last objective is to add a feature so that more than one player can join the same game and attempt the same set of questions to increase the level of competition. However, this is not prioritized and only serves as a potential future update concept if we choose to expand our product further, because we fear that there would not be sufficient time to incorporate this into our game before the deadline comes, and the methods to fully implement it would be too complex for us to handle now. Just to briefly explain, this feature would allow up to four players to enter a game together as four separate teams and consequently will have to race one another to “hit the buzzer”, which allows that player an opportunity to answer. If their answer turns out to be correct, that person will take all the points of that question, and if they choose the wrong option, the remaining competitors have another chance to “hit the buzzer” and show their answer to that question. In the scenario that no one can provide an answer after 30 seconds, the number points of that particular question will be extinguished and everyone will carry on to the following questions. The lifelines mentioned in the single player mode will be disabled in this version of “The Ultimate Trivia Game”, because this mode encourages competition and speed without hints. By the end of the round, whoever gets the highest amount of points is declared the winner of the game.  
In order for us to achieve the pinnacle of success for this final project, our team must devise a thorough plan with distinct Phases guiding us through each and every task based on our general aim and specific objectives. On top of all that, we need a way to schedule this working plan in a logical and systematic way, with each person handling certain parts of the project with different deadlines and the whole group will come together and the end of each week to recount the finished work in comparison to the overall progress, as well as making any adjustments or evaluations if necessary. In order for our plan to be efficient, we will be incorporating the “divide and conquer” strategy that we learned during our courses at RMIT, which basically means decomposing a large-scale assignment like this into smaller problems that are much easier to complete.   For instance, if our initial goal for week X (with X being the week number in the semester) was to complete the basic features of our game and add 10 questions to the game to test out the mechanism, then we would probably set 1-2 days for finding the questions, and 2-3 days to build the bare-bone version for testing. If all goes well according to the plan, by the end of week X, we expect to have fulfilled these tasks and prepare for the next week with new goals. On the other hand, if we were not able to meet those requirements within the allotted timeframe, then our group will organize a meeting (either face-to-face or via an online platform) where the person in charge of that task will explain why he could not complete it in time and what were the hindrances he faced. After that, our group will collectively come up with a solution, and this could be to pick up the pace in the following weeks, remove a feature completely or just modifying our plan depending on current circumstances. To sum up, our team’s plan is quite structured and is re-designed on a weekly basis, which allows us more flexibility when encountering unwanted incidents or setbacks, as opposed to scheduling the whole project in one go and hoping that there would not be unexpected occurrences. This style of working guarantees that we will complete the project one task at a time, slowly and steadily.

   To be more specific, our general plan for the entirety of this assignment is divided into three distinct Phases. Phase 1 is where we will write the whole report for this project, Phase 2 includes creating our official website and refine it to our own preferences before pushing the information from our report into our site, while Phase 3 and 4 consis  ts of building a fully-functional product (or at least a working demonstration) and making PowerPoint slides to prepare for our presentation respectively. The great thing about our system is that the priority of the three Phases are interchangeable, meaning that we can choose to shuffle the order of these Phases to satisfy unexpected elements like time constraints, errors, conflicts, contribution problems, changes in requirements and the availability of the technology we will be using.  Unlike most of the groups in this course, which were formed significantly early into the semester and therefore had more than enough time to settle on an idea, our team was only officially assembled by the end of the ninth week. This was an unfortunate setback that left us about less than three weeks to finish this project to the best of our abilities, and it was a great challenge indeed. We definitely struggled at first for obvious reasons, mainly because none of us knew each other so communication was a huge barrier. After our group was able to “break the ice”, we immediately had to organize several meetings to come up with and agree on a project idea. The sense of urgency due to impending deadlines was very strenuous, and we had to brainstorm constantly to think of good ideas. Luckily, we stumbled upon the perfect topic to work on since everyone showed high enthusiasm towards it, and tasks were assigned to each member of the team to start working at once. Thanks to quick thinking from the entire group, we managed to save lots of precious time conceptualizing and can begin the physical processes almost instantly.   The beginning steps in our working progress together was also not seamless at all, and we had to surpass many obstacles before we could get comfortable with working as a unit. One of the most serious problems we have encountered was to search for the appropriate software to develop our game, since the documentation for constructing a trivia game was much harder to come by than we previously expected. Another issue was simply slight miscommunication between members, which led to misinterpretation of ideas. This conflict was quickly resolved by creating a private group chat so that our team can easily converse and exchange thoughts, and by making shared accounts so that each member can upload their individual workflow on for the rest of the team to see. After a bumpy start to this important assignment, we quickly collected ourselves and picked up the pace of our tasks, and the overall efficiency of “the IT squad” witnessed a tremendous leap in a positive way.
Apart from the difficulties above, our group have yet to face any severe dead-ends concerning our project. All of the troubles we faced throughout the assignment were dealt with swiftly and professionally, by evaluating our options in terms of their pros and cons and eliminating liabilities to the success of our project. Having said that, we did not need to make too many decisions or changes to the features of our game, so the initial plan we discussed above is still mostly intact. One major modification we had to make during this project was to switch to an alternative way of developing our trivia game entirely. Our initial intentions were to program a fully-operational trivia game from top to bottom using the built-in library Pygame available in Pycharm Pro. It was definitely an ambitious mission for us and we soon realized that it exceeded our level of competence at the present, and also learning the necessary knowledge would be detrimentally impeding on our time for other crucial aspects of this assignment. When we all came to an agreement that this route was no longer ideal, our team did not have any choice but to return to square 1, which was to determine the means to carry out our project. Each member of our group browsed the web for days in hope of finding a viable solution, and eventually our group leader Khánh discovered a perfect tool for us – Trivia Maker. This platform is much more suitable for our particular requirements, and its resources are extremely well-supported with tutorials to guide us through the game-making procedure. Furthermore, Trivia Maker is fairly simple to operate and has features where you can custom and design a unique user interface for your game instead of selecting a preset template that they provide. To sum up, we had a big problem in developing our game and even had to reset our progress, but everyone pulled through as a team and we ended up using a substitute which was better for us compared to our first choice.
