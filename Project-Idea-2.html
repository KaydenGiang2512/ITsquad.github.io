SCOPE AND LIMITS

The purpose of our project idea and also the underlying message that we want to convey to our potential customers is that you can indeed learn new things just by participating in a fun activity without any form of consequences. Moreover, we are trying to promote interactive education through our free platform and hopefully spark the interest of young people to always seek out new knowledge. Having defined the end goal of this assignment, the primary deliverables our team are going for include an operational version of “The Ultimate Trivia Game” loaded with approximately 150-200 questions from a variety of genres. When a user launches our game on their devices, there should be a home page (with an accompanying theme song if possible) with a start button in the middle and the game title “The Ultimate Trivia Game” on top, a control panel containing buttons (sounds button, music button, high scores button, home button) displaying on the right-hand corner of the screen.   The moment the player clicks “Start” which happens to be our logo, they will be redirected to a page where the rules of the game are explained in clear detail. Once they have mastered the rules, just click the “Continue” button below the text box to move on to the actual gameplay and questions. A question page typically has a large text box centralized in the screen containing the prompt, and four rectangular boxes each showing a possible answer. Choose the right option, and you will be awarded with points and proceed to the following question. Otherwise, a message will pop up revealing the correct choice and will deduct points from your total. Your overall score will be announced at the end of a round and saved into the high scores table. If all of the goals and outcomes are accurately met, then it can be concluded that this project was a rousing success that can be publicly released for our targeted demographics to enjoy. 

  As a disclaimer, “The Ultimate Trivia Game” does not support some online features such as live competitions, real-life rewards, global leaderboards, trivia races or anything of that sort. Additionally, this game will only be available in single player mode for the time being as stated in the previous sections. Because having multiple players in the same game would be too complicated to pull off, this will be categorized as beyond the scope of our project. Lastly, our trivia game will not offer in-app purchases for tokens or power-ups of any kind, as it would violate our initial purpose of a pure test of knowledge with only three lifelines to guide you through the questions instead of more fancy add-ins components. Most of the features we decided to opt out from our game is due to personal preferences and to a lesser extent, time constraints of this assignment. This is still our first semester as IT students, so we needed to set boundaries of what should be incorporated instead of being too ambitous and coming up short in the end.

On account of the simplicity of our project idea, the software requirements that we need are completely accessible and is free of charge for the most part. To compile our team report, Microsoft Word alone is sufficient because it has all the tools and technologies particularly for writing, and because we all have the official license and years of experience so there is virtually no other suitable alternative. For our website, we will be using Visual Studio Code to program every aspect of it as well as pushing the finished code to our online repository via Git. We chose this software because it is cost-free and we are all quite familiar with how to use VS Code thanks to the tutorial sessions we attended. Last but not least, our group will be constructing “The Ultimate Trivia Game” using the Trivia Maker platform, and we even purchased the premium version for an affordable 6.99$ to gain access to all features unlimited. Out of all the tools we are incorporating, Trivia Maker is the only platform where none of us have previously experimented with. However, this minor detail would not affect us too much because this platform is well-documented with comprehensive tutorials to help us navigate through all the functions of this website in a matter of minutes. In essence, our group will be making this game on the foundation of the technologies we are accustomed to in class, which decreases the time spent on understanding the tools we need to use and prevents us from being distracted from our other objectives.

This is one of the most exciting procedures of any project and yet is one of the most terrifying, simply because it will determine whether our working efforts were fruitful or were they in vain. If the product we created passed these tests with flying colors, this means that we are heading on the right track and our game is performing astoundingly on all cylinders. On the other hand, if our tests return imperfections, then we would immediately look for those errors to eliminate them right away. Our group intend to test our game out after every input session, which means that everytime we finished a brand-new feature we would run the game from the beginning up to our most recent modification to check for inaccuracies and resolve them (if any). This area will be named “Alpha Testing”, where we as the developers try out all aspects concerning our game before the official release to the public.   After our product has passed the “Alpha Testing” period, we will proceed with the “Beta Testing” process where our team will search for random participants to try out the first version of “The Ultimate Trivia Maker” and in turn, they will give us their comments and feedback on their overall experience, as well as provide us with suggestions/recommendations for enhancement. We will probably need a sample size of around 10-20 people including our IT friends, lecturers (if possible) and also non-IT friends to receive as transparent and objective opinions as possible. It is crucial for this “Beta Testing” stage to be as randomized as possible, since these peer reviews of the game will greatly serve us in terms of bug fixes and optimizations in preparation for future updates. Therefore, we need to invite students and faculty members from different areas of expertise to play our game for a wider exposure to many demographics, consequently receiving more generalized and multi-dimensional perspectives.

RISKS

Conducting a large-scale project such as this means that we need to conscious of certain risks and threats towards the eventual success of this assignment. Once our group has made ourselves aware of these imminent problems, we need to devise proper counter-measures to prevent them from affecting our collective performance. Since our topic choice involves developing a trivia game, there are quite a few technical risks that comes to our attention, especially when each individual has reasonable experience with the current gaming market. Some threats include fatal crashes or errors that make a game severely malfunction, inaccuracies in the information provided (outdated data, wrong statistics, bad questions), insufficient time to complete the basic mechanism, software and hardware issues (system failure, old version, lack of supporting documents, computer crashes, data loss). After a long period of brainstorming, these were several liabilities we could come up with that may happen during the developing process, but it can be assumed that more may arise unexpectedl and beyond our control. Apart from the technological aspect, we should also prepare for more general risks such as poor team communication, delay in tasks, conflicts or unwanted incidents from any of the team members. To sum up, we have assessed and anticipated various issues that could hinder our progress to the best of our knowledge and will look out for them during our working period and the whole team will be cautious in conducting this project for guaranteed success.

Group proccesses and communications

  A group cannot operate adequately without strong communication between members and every decision must be made unanimously under the approval of each member. Thankfully, the introduction of social media platforms like Facebook, Discord, Gmail, Zalo or Skype has provided us with much more convenience in organizing virtual meetings regardless of location or time, as opposed to just the traditional face-to-face meetings. Nevertheless, physical meetings are still our preferable style of team meeting, so we would make every opportunity to host at least one face-to-face meeting per week. If this option cannot be done, then social media is a viable solution, and we can easily exchange documents and ideas by chatting on Messenger/Discord or uploading files to our shared Github or Gmail account. Moreover, we could even arrange video calls every weekend to have more interaction and for each person to have a chance to discuss their progress with the rest of the team. Because our group appreciates close-knit teamwork and always try to promote communication, we all expect each other to actively share their thoughts and opinions in the chats on a daily basis so that we can stay connected and possibly learn new things about each other. As a result, our productivity will increase exponentially which in turn will lead to desirable outcomes. We all have discipline as individuals so working together means mutual respect for one another and quick responses to team announcements. Should a team member show laziness in this aspect, we would definitely try to solve this conflict amongst ourselves first and only when things spiral out of hand will we take this problem to our lecturer for his final decision.
